Banaras (Varanasi)


Banaras, also known as Kashi, is one of the world's oldest living cities and the spiritual heart of India. Located on the banks of the holy Ganges River, it is famous for its labyrinthine alleys, ancient temples, and the vibrant cultural heritage that defines the essence of Indian spirituality.

Top 5 Attractions

  1. Dashashwamedh Ghat: The busiest ghat where the mesmerizing evening Ganga Aarti takes place daily.
  2. Shri Kashi Vishwanath Temple: A historic and sacred shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its golden spires.
  3. Sarnath: The peaceful pilgrimage site where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon.
  4. Manikarnika Ghat: A place of deep significance where visitors can witness the eternal cycle of life and death.
  5. Assi Ghat: A popular spot for morning yoga and watching the sunrise over the Ganges.

Must-Try Foods

  • Kachori Sabzi: A classic breakfast consisting of crispy deep-fried bread served with spicy potato curry.
  • Tamatar Chaat: A unique local street food made with spiced mashed tomatoes and crunchy sev.
  • Banarasi Paan: The world-famous betel leaf preparation enjoyed as a refreshing mouth freshener.
  • Malaiyyo: A light and airy saffron-flavored milk froth that is a seasonal winter delicacy.
  • Rabri Jalebi: A heavenly combination of hot, syrupy jalebis served with thick, chilled cream.
